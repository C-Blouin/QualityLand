About the Book

"QualityLand" is a satirical science fiction novel that depicts a futuristic dystopian society ruled by consumerism, technology, and corporate overlords. The foundation of Qualityland utilizes a foolproof universal ranking algorithm system developed by the most successful company in the world, TheShop, to determine opportunities individuals receive based on their quality scores, value, and contributions. The story follows Peter Jobless, an ordinary citizen who unwittingly becomes a figure of resistance in a world where individuals are treated based solely on their quality scorings. Furthermore, Peter embarks on a journey that challenges the dehumanizing forces of this hyper-connected society, raising questions about identity, privacy, and the pervasive influence of technology and consumerism in our digital age. The book employs dark humour and satire to critique prominent societal issues while providing insightful thoughts about our digital behaviours.

Themes of QualityLand

Consumerism & Branding

The book touches on the influences consumerism and branding have on humanity. It raises questions regarding our identities and how the products we buy and brands we align with define an individual's status. This theme is relevant to our digital personas through online advertisements, e-commerce, and the influences of social media outlets.

Technological Consequences

QualityLand sheds light on the consequences of moving forward in the digital age alongside our technological dependencies, highlighting the impacts of Artificial Intelligence and loss of privacy. Further, the book serves as a cautionary tale of the significant issues technology has brought to the current digital age.

Inequalities

QualityLand portrays inequalities in the digital world. The book depicts a society where individuals' Quality scores determine their treatment and status. Further, it creates relatable concerns to our digital norms and how an individual's social or economic status determines their value and offering to the world.

Identity and Self-Reflection

The story promotes the theme of individuals in search of authenticity. It does so through the character of Peter Jobless, revealing self-discovery themes of a world dominated by digital personas and AI. The book provides readers an opportunity for self-reflection on personal values and to understand who they are beyond their digital profiles.

Meet the Author

Marc-Uwe Kling

Marc-Uwe Kling is a German bestselling author known for his satirical and humorous works. Born on July 31, 1982, in Stuttgart, Germany, he is a multi-talented artist with a unique ability to blend sharp humour with a witty approach resonating with many individuals. Furthermore, his writing touches on problematic social issues, critiquing topics such as consumerism and the impact of technological advancements on the lives of individuals. He has established himself as an impactful voice in the literary world through his famous work QualityLand and has earned valuable awards for his contributions.

More About Marc-Uwe Kling

Kling studied philosophy and theatre at Humboldt University in Berlin, providing a foundation for creating memorable experiences through humour and satire.

Marc-Uwe Kling's writing style focused on a witty and clever approach, with well-thought-out wordplay accompanying a sharp tone. Kling demonstrates exposure to absurd and harsh realities of modern life portrayed with a touch of satire, expressing various aspects of life while creating relatable experiences.

Renowned throughout the literary world for his acclaimed work QualityLand is a science fiction novel set in a futuristic society where technology and algorithms determine various aspects of an individual's life. QualityLand explores themes of consumerism and technology through the lens of TheShop, the largest corporation, providing insightful development impacts moving into the inevitable technological age.

Since Marc-Uwe Kling is known for his extraordinary storytelling talent through satire, he presents himself on the radio or performing live shows, which led to Kling earning the Kleinkunstpreis Baden-Württemberg (Cabaret & Comedy) and Salzburger Stier (Radio Comedy) awards.

  • Best-seller Author: Several of Kling's books, including "Qualityland" and "The Kangaroo Chronicles," have become bestsellers in Germany alongside other countries. His unique writing style resonates with readers from all over the world.
  • German Science Fiction Award: Kling received this award for his well-recognized novel QualityLand, which touches on key themes of consumerism, automation, and artificial intelligence throughout a futuristic dystopian world.
  • Erich Kästner Prize for Literature: Kling was awarded the Erich Kästner Prize, named after the famous German author Erich Kästner, for his contributions to contemporary literature.
